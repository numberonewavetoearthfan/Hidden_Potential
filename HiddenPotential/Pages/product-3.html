Items Needed

  • Paint

How This Product Helps

Using plastic spoons for crafts, even if not a perfect hibiscus, is a great way to help the planet by giving trash a new purpose.

Step By Step Tutorial

  • Cut Petals: Cut 5-7 heart-shaped or teardrop-shaped petals for each flower. You might cut some slightly larger and some slightly smaller for layering.
  • Shape Petals (Optional for Fabric): For a more realistic look, you can gently curl the edges of fabric petals using a candle flame (very carefully and quickly!) or a shaping tool.
  • Assemble Flower: Layer the petals, starting with the largest ones at the bottom, overlapping them to form a circular flower shape. Secure each layer with a dot of hot glue in the center.
  • Add Center: Glue a bundle of yellow or white stamens into the center of the flower.
  • Attach to Clip: Once the flower is assembled and dry, hot glue it securely to a hair clip blank.
